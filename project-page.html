About this portfolio

Design Rationale

The primary goal of this portfolio was to truly represent who I am. While I’m proud beyond belief that I’m known as “The Google Guy” in my career, I want to be known for who I truly am. Sure, you could show only who you are from a technical perspective, but if you knew more about my life --what I love, and who I strive to be-- then when that “perfect match” is made with a potential employer I know I will be able to make that impact on the world. Not only would I have a fulfilling career, but finally be able to make that difference in the world. That’s where the primary influence in the design originated: why I chose to include my writing, gaming, and more aspects about my life and who I am beyond the resume.
I knew that potential users of this portfolio would be interested in who I am; whether it be potential employers or colleagues. When viewing a portfolio, you’re learning more about a person beyond their resume and career experiences. To create an easy and interactive user-experience, I stuck close to a simple and linear design, employing light blues and greens paired with whites and grays, as well as selecting imagery that aptly represented the “high tech” material with easy to grasp icons for guidance. Navigation shouldn’t be complex for the sake of seeming impressive, but rather its simplicity can help to aptly represent the message I’m trying to send. By using "cards" and a row-column structure, the site easily maintains a sense of consistency, alignment, and a focused purpose.
